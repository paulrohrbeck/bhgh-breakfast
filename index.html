About

Join us at the “Success Starts With Hope” Breakfast for Boys Hope Girls Hope at the Olympic Club in downtown San Francisco. This is a FREE breakfast for people to come and learn more about Hope Academy and the children we serve. It is a fundraiser. You will be asked to consider making a contribution at the event but you will not be required to give. As much as anything, we want you to come and find out about the profound impact Hope Academy has on the lives of the children-in-need in our community.

To attend this event, please RSVP and Anne Nguyen will follow up with you.

Boys Hope Girls Hope San Francisco Bay Area (BHGH-SF Bay Area) is a college preparatory and life enrichment program that serves academically motivated young people in situations of significant need. Since opening our doors in May 2000, we have helped dozens of young children—our scholars—who have the potential to succeed academically but often live in challenging situations and/or communities where the resources and attention to help them succeed are not available.

Through our Hope Academy, a community-based program, we provide academic support and enrichment through a nurturing and structured environment and consistent and comprehensive care. This approach maximizes their chances for a successful journey to—and through—college. Importantly, 100% of our students come from families living at or below poverty line, and all of our collegians are the very first in their families to go to college.

For more information, please visit our website at www.bhghsf.org.

Panelists

Join us in a rich dialogue about providing access to higher education to all.

Geralyn Yparraguirre, MA

Assistant Director of Undergraduate Admissions at UC Berkeley

Ms. Yparraguirre is dedicated to improving the educational pipeline for underrepresented students who want to attend college. Her passion for college readiness is evident in her extensive experience in educational outreach. She works with high schools, community colleges, and educational organizations to improve programmatic and student services to college-bound students.

At UC Berkeley, Ms. Yparraguirre personally completed comprehensive review of over 7,800 applications for undergraduate admissions. As the Assistant Director, she manages regional outreach efforts for over 20 organizations, serves as one of our Diversity Outreach Coordinators for underrepresented communities, and Department Liaison to the Haas School of Business.

Dr. Jacob Perea

Dean Emeritus at San Francisco State University

Having served as Dean for 18 years, Dr. Jacob Perea is Dean Emeritus of the Graduate College of Education at San Francisco State University, and a Professor of Equity, Leadership Studies, and Instructional Technologies.

Dr. Perea has taught courses at San Francisco State University since 1975, in both the Graduate College of Education and the College of Ethnic Studies. Dr. Perea’s primary research interest is in student K-12 and higher educational success. He is the founder of the Step To College Program, a collaborative project with Bay Area high schools focusing on encouraging minority students to attend and remain in college, and the Chinese Masters and Credential Program. He presently teaches in the Transfer Articulation Bridge outreach program at City College of San Francisco, the Step to College program for high school students, and the Chinese Masters and Credential program housed in ELSIT.

Dr. Perea has been honored by the Mayor of San Francisco and the Board of Supervisors for his work with the Step to College Program. He was recognized by KQED and the Bank of America as an “Unsung Hero” for his work with youth in San Francisco and was celebrated by the Meiklejohn Civil Liberties Institute of Berkeley as an outstanding education activist.

David Montesano

Admission Strategist at College Match, Inc.

As an admissions strategist, David has helped hundreds of clients find and gain access to the best match undergraduate and graduate programs over the past ten years. From offices in the U.S., David serves families with students who hope to attend college from a wide range of achievement levels and high school backgrounds.

Dave is also the author of “Strategic College Admission”, published in CollegeBound News (April, 2005). Profiled by Washington Post education writer, Jay Matthews, in Newsweek’s “America’s Hottest Colleges” (2005 edition), David was called a “new breed of college admission consultants who use business school marketing principles to sell students to their preferred college”.

Julie Allen

Vice President and Chief Academic Officer at BHGH

Julie Allen is the Chief Academic Officer at Boys Hope Girls Hope (BHGH) since 2002, and was named Vice President in early 2014.

Ms. Allen began her career in education as an artist-in-residence for the Denver Public Schools, earning recognition from the Rockefeller Foundation for Excellence in Arts Education. Upon moving from the classroom to educational administration, Ms. Allen served as Director of Education for Denver Community Schools. She was twice recognized by the Colorado Association of Community Educators for innovative school and community partnerships that resulted in dramatically improved educational outcomes for urban students.

From 1998 to 2002, she was Director of the Midwest Consortium for Service-Learning in Higher Education at the University of Nebraska-Lincoln where she guided twenty-two higher education institutions in implementation of policies and programs that promoted a sustainable infrastructure for civic engagement and service-learning. While at UNL, Ms. Allen conceptualized and implemented the nationally recognized Nebraska Teacher Corps, a “teachers as scholars” project involving over 1,500 classroom teachers. The success of these efforts resulted in recognition for outstanding contributions to students and teaching from the University of Nebraska Teaching Council.
